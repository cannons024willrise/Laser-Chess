How to Play

Simple rules and game flow

01 The pieces

KING

Goal

This is the piece you are trying to protect. It has no shield, so if a laser hits any side, it is removed and the game ends right away.

[ Fixed in place ]
Always vulnerable [ Tap to see example ]

SWITCH

Special

This piece has mirrors on both sides, so it cannot be removed. It can also swap places with a nearby Defender or Deflector without turning.

Cannot be removed [ Tap to see example ]

DEFENDER

Shield

The front side blocks lasers. It stays on the board when hit from the front, but is removed if hit from the side or back.

Weak on the sides and back [ Tap to see example ]

DEFLECTOR

Mirror

One side reflects the laser at a right angle. If the beam hits any of the other three sides, the piece is removed.

Weak on three sides [ Tap to see example ]

LASER

Laser

A fixed beam source in the corner. It cannot move or be removed, but it can be rotated to fire down the first column or across the first row.

Always active [ Tap to see example ]

02 On your turn

Choose one of these actions:

Move

Move any piece one square in any direction, including diagonals. The piece keeps facing the same way.

Rotate

Turn any piece 90° clockwise or counterclockwise while it stays on the same square.

Swap

The Switch can trade places with a nearby Defender or Deflector of either color.

03 Board rules

!

Blue pieces cannot move onto squares marked with Red Helix symbols. Red pieces cannot move onto squares marked with Blue Helix symbols.

!

No take-backs. Once you let go of a piece, the move is locked in and the laser fires automatically.

04 Laser fires automatically

After each turn:

Your laser fires automatically after every turn. The beam bounces off mirrors until it leaves the board or hits a piece. Any piece hit on a non-mirrored side is removed. You can rotate your laser before it fires.

Your own beam can remove your pieces too.

05 No endless loops

If the same position repeats three times:

The rule

If the exact same board position happens three times in one match, the game ends in a draw.

What counts as the same position

A position only counts as repeated if every surviving piece is on the same square, facing the same direction, and it is the same player's turn.
